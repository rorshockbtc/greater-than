copy, modify, and redistribute the software. Trademark law is separate
from copyright law, and the licence does **not** grant you rights to
the names, logos, or brand identity associated with the project.

- **ColonHyphenBracket** and **ColonHyphenBracket Studio** — the name

## Permitted use

You **may** use the marks, without prior permission, to:

- Truthfully describe that your software is built on, forked from,
  derived from, or compatible with Greater. Examples:
  *"Powered by Greater"*, *"A fork of Greater for the
  `<your-niche>` industry"*, *"Compatible with Greater's catalog
  format"*.
- Refer to Greater in academic writing, journalism, talks, blog
  posts, comparison reviews, and similar nominative contexts.
- Display the project name on attribution pages, in changelogs, and
  in licence notices as required by the MIT licence.

## Restricted use

You **may not**, without prior written permission:

- Distribute a modified or rebranded fork of this software under the
  name **Greater**, or under any name confusingly similar to
  Greater, ColonHyphenBracket, or `:-]`.
- Use the marks in a way that suggests endorsement, sponsorship, or
  affiliation with the original project where none exists.
- Use the marks in domain names, application names, or company
  names whose primary purpose is to compete with or impersonate the
  original project.
- Use the hot-pink-on-dark visual identity, the `:-]` wordmark, or
  the `colonhyphenbracket.pink` domain branding in marketing
  material for a forked product.

If you fork Greater and ship it as a product, **please rename it**.
Suggested pattern: `<your-name>-bot` built on the Greater shell. The
attribution can — and should — say so.

## Why this matters

Greater is a small open-source project that depends on its name and
identity to differentiate from vendor chatbots and from generic-AI
forks. A rebrand-and-resell of the unmodified shell under the
"Greater" name would dilute that identity and confuse the people
the project is trying to reach. The MIT licence covers the code; this
policy covers the name.
